# Examination Questions

## Availability

The USAWC oral comprehensive examination uses a slate of 12 questions covering all seven Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs). The question slate rotates annually.


**AY26 questions:** 

Consult the three-part rubric. A successful answer will integrate theories, models, or concepts from at least two courses you studied in your USAWC curriculum and will be supported by historical or recent examples of your choice.

1.Evaluate how military organizational culture influences significant change initiatives.

2.Evaluate the implications of ethical failure by strategic military leaders.

3.Analyze the enduring strategic tensions within defense modernization <in America or your country>.

4.Evaluate the sufficiency of <America’s or your country’s> military force structure to meet <America’s or your country’s> emerging national security challenges.

5.Assess the unique contributions and limitations of landpower in joint warfighting across domains in the Indo-Pacific theater.

6.Describe how operational art is applied in designing joint campaigns that gain positions of relative advantage in armed conflict.

7.Explain how joint campaigns should be designed to ensure a favorable post-conflict environment.

8.Critique a current U.S. campaign or operation in terms of operational approach, resource alignment, and risk management.

9.Design a U.S. strategy to prevent a hostile foreign power from gaining an economic or military foothold in the Western Hemisphere consistent with the priorities outlined in the 2025 NSS.

10.Select an active conflict and analyze the role of landpower in achieving war termination.

11.Evaluate the strategic options available to an allied or partner nation facing a significant current threat to national security.

12.Select a geographic combatant command and analyze how the US should confront the most significant threat to its national security emanating from that region.

**Program Learning Outcomes:** 
1. Develop options for employing landpower in joint warfighting.

2. Apply strategic thinking to analyze current and future national security and strategic military challenges.

3. Design strategies using analytical frameworks and theories to address national security challenges across the competition continuum.

4. Integrate military and non-military instruments of national power to pursue national interests.

5. Identify the military requirements of current and future security environments.

6. Apply strategic leadership principles and theories to achieve sustained organizational performance.

7. Demonstrate clear and concise communication of national security challenges appropriate to audiences, purpose, and context.

## For Replicating Institutions

You do not need USAWC questions to run MilBench. Use your own institution's capstone examination questions. The protocol requires only that questions:

1. Demand integration across at least two disciplines in the curriculum.
2. Are open-ended and require sustained analysis (not factual recall).
3. Allow for impromptu faculty follow-up questioning.

If you wish to compare results directly with the USAWC pilot, contact the authors to discuss question alignment.
